<overview>
Agent-readable pages are retrieval units. They should give a cold agent enough context to understand the concept, cite the right source, follow links, and know when the page is not authoritative enough.
</overview>

<page_types>
**Concept page:** answers "what is the rule, object, process, or fact?" Use for one durable topic. It opens with context, states the rule, cites sources, links adjacent concepts, and avoids restating facts owned elsewhere.

**Index page:** answers "what lives in this section and where should I start?" Use for roots, sections, personas, and source families. It orients and lists structural children with meaningful descriptions.

**Use-case page:** answers "what do I do in this situation?" Use for recurring operator jobs. It links to concept pages for authority rather than restating all doctrine.

**Synthesis page:** compares options, maps decisions, or joins multiple concepts. It should name its source pages and stay aware that it is not the canonical home for every underlying fact.

**Frontier page:** tracks moving developments. It needs `as_of`, expected decay, refresh triggers, and prominent caveats.

**Gap page:** lists known missing coverage, unresolved questions, or source limitations. It is an honesty mechanism and a roadmap.
</page_types>

<cold_readable_shape>
A concept page should normally include:

1. A short opening that situates the concept in the domain.
2. The durable rule or fact pattern, with source-backed claims.
3. Important exceptions, thresholds, dates, or decision points.
4. Links to canonical homes of adjacent concepts.
5. A source list or frontmatter sources.
6. Status and freshness metadata.

Avoid openings like "This page covers..." when the first sentence can state the actual concept.
</cold_readable_shape>

<linking_style>
Use link text that helps a cold reader. Prefer "the approval threshold for restricted transfers" over "see transfer rules". Link the first meaningful mention of a concept that has its own file. Do not rely on "see also" lists to carry all relationships.
</linking_style>

<duplication_rule>
Before adding a paragraph, ask:

- Is this the canonical home for the fact?
- If not, should this page link to the canonical home instead?
- If the fact must be repeated for usability, does it clearly point back to the canonical home?
</duplication_rule>
